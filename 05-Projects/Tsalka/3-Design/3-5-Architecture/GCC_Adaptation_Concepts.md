# Architectural Adaptations for GCC Market: Concept Sketches

To appeal to the GCC/Saudi market, specific architectural modifications focus on **Visual Privacy** and **Functional Separation** of guest/family areas.

## 1. Absolute Visual Privacy (Exterior)
For the *Grand Villa* and *Comfort+* units, the pool area must be completely shielded from neighbors and passersby.

**Concept:**
*   **High Walls**: 2.5m+ natural stone walls.
*   **Green Screens**: Dense row of Cypress or Arborvitae trees.
*   **Result**: A "Private Sanctuary" feeling.

## 2. Functional Separation (Interior)

### A. The "Majlis" (Formal Guest Reception)
This is a formal area for hosting male guests or business associates. It remains separate from the rest of the house to protect the privacy of the female family members.

**Key Features:**
*   Separate entrance (if possible).
*   Formal seating arrangement (U-shape).
*   Luxurious "Showpiece" design.

![Formal Majlis Interior](/C:/Users/Nodar/.gemini/antigravity/brain/f7c0a645-f001-4fec-af9a-8c67c4f0ffed/gcc_majlis_interior_1770144499131.png)

### B. The Family Living Room (Private)
This is the heart of the home, strictly for the husband, wife, and children.

**Key Features:**
*   Cozy, relaxed atmosphere.
*   Direct access to the private garden/pool.
*   Hidden from the Majlis entrance.
